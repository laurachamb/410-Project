2016 Reflections: Greetings from a cozy fire surrounded by loving friends in Santa Fe.

Gratitude does not begin to explain how my heart feels for so many of you. For me, 2015-16 was a self-imposed adventure of a lifetime; connecting the most passionate and brilliant people trying to make the world a better place from all sectors - philanthropy, nonprofits, for profits, public, and funds with each other.

For those of you who are wondering if there are enough people trying and caring; fear not. These changemakers might not make the news as much as they deserve, but just come hang out with our growing 40+ person team now based in CO, WA, IN, and VA to both meet and find ways to support these inspiring changemakers.

The journey was one of being willing to fail in every way, having a big idea and believing it, asking hundreds of people to help with that idea, learning to "receive," and learning to lead a team of literal rock stars - many of them more experienced, brilliant, and more wise than me.

I said goodbye to self care, which is not recommended. I relied on so many of you to hold me up literally with food, shelter, clothing, and countless words of encouragement and you are still here - now showing up to support that shift for me back into self care - sleep, exercise, cooking, and opening up my heart for love again. I am much more likely to take a meeting with you if you suggest we meet over a walk, hike, or bike ride.

There was a moment in February 2016 after running around the state of Colorado and giving of 50 presentations and workshops and maxing out almost every credit card that I was pretty certain I would go personally bankrupt and experience the biggest public failure of my life. The professionals recommended that we cancel CO Impact Days a month out.

Who could blame them? There were no indicators that gave us even chance of succeeding.

I will never forget the feeling like I was in the dark at the edge of a really high cliff. I was literally surrounded by a team that believed for the vision? me? - that team not only held my hand; they pulled and pushed me over the edge jumping right along side me. To those of you who jumped with me, all, I can say is that if you ever find yourself at a cliff, I hope that I will be there holding your hand and jumping right along side of you.

Doug, Greg, Karen, Molly, Molly, Kerianne, Vic, Brynne, Nicole, Nicole, Dom, Tony, Cindy, Steffanie, Luis, Ted, Heather, Ceyl, Katherine, Kara, Kim, Jason, Dylan, Ella, Jim, Mel, Diane, Andrew, Jeremy, Mark, Heather, Callie, Pat, Gideon, Martha, Will, David, John, Pat, Joanne, Kathy, John, John, Michael, Mel, Jenn, Wynne, Doug, Zia, Jeff, Kate, Molly, Patricia, Pat, Helen, Holmes, Andrew, and Charles, Bob, Caryn, Rick, Gaye, John, Jacqui, Devin, and the other 750 people who joined either the CO Impact Initiative and Days events as volunteers or participants. THANK you.

Go BIG or go home. Over 20 impact investments have been made as a result of this effort. Yes, we came in short $150,000, but I did not go personally bankrupt and we have even bolder and bigger dreams with both our long-term and new friends and partners for 2017. There is no question that building on what we did last year seems so much less scary - expanding upon a dream versus birthing a dream to life.

For those of you seeking a little inspiration, I wanted to share some of the letters we keep in our "Love Letters Folder." I hope they inspire you the way they inspire us and if you want to join the movement, let us know!

We will be launching a Colorado tour of 15+ towns and delivering 2 days of workshops and presentations and a National Impact Investing Marketplace tour presenting in major cities. If you want to join in on the fun, please sign up at http://www.coimpactdays.org/signup. We believe we can grow what we have started in Colorado regionally and replicate it nationally and we will only be able to do it with help from people like you.

CO Impact Days was the first statewide marketplace for impact investing: it successfully identified, connected, and celebrated Colorado's top social ventures and impact investors from the Western Slope to the Front Range. CO Impact Days’ education, symposium, marketplace, awards, and celebration were designed to bring together Colorado’s top 60 social ventures, 200+ impact investors, and 500+ community members in a first of its kind, 2-day, inspiring event to Learn. Connect. Invest.

The CO Impact Initiative, which includes CO Impact Days, is the larger 3-year strategy to elevate and accelerate impact investing in Colorado, catalyzing the flow of $100 million in investment capital into Colorado social ventures that deliver positive impact on the state’s communities, economy and natural environment.
