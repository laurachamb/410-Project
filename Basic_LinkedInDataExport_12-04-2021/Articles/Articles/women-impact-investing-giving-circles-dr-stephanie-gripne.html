Created on 2017-01-20 21:25

Published on 2017-01-20 22:17

How many projects, nonprofits, for profits, or funds led by or benefiting women need capital?

Let's find out and resource them.

Those of you in DC on Sunday 1/22 or Baltimore on 1/23 - please join Dr. Maya Rockeymoore and me to learn about a new tool, "Impact Investing Giving Circles" that you can use in partnership with your local community foundation to mobilize individuals and institutions to support women led social ventures.

The Impact Finance Center believes that one of the key strategies for increasing the flow of philanthropic capital into impact investing opportunities is to create safe first steps or stepping stones that allow a conventional philanthropist or investor to experience impact investing in a low cost and safe way. The Impact Finance Center is developing a series of experiential learning opportunities such as “Impact Investing Giving Circles,” where philanthropists or investors would donate into a charitable fund similar to a Donor Advised Fund or Communities of Interest Fund to collaborate on impact investing. The donor would receive a tax deduction into the fund and the fund would make loans or equity investments to projects, nonprofits, for profits, or funds that further the charitable purpose of the donor.
